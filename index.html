Teaser

TL;DR

We introduce a bounded push–pull objective in diffusion cross-attention space that removes target concepts while limiting semantic drift, improving the erasure-preservation trade-off for closely related concepts.

Abstract

Rapid advances in diffusion models have raised concerns about privacy, harmful content, and copyright violations. Concept erasure methods aim to address these risks by removing undesirable concepts from pretrained models without full retraining. However, existing approaches often degrade non-target generation quality through heuristic strength scaling such as classifier-free guidance or negative prompt scaling, leading to uncontrolled and excessive semantic drift.

To address this, we propose Push–Pull Attentional Anchoring, a mechanism in the cross-attention space that displaces target representations while anchoring the scale of erasure via bounded cosine-similarity constraints. Our margin operates on the relative cosine ratio to the original model rather than an absolute difference, yielding an adaptive and concept-aware distance threshold calibrated by each layer's original target-anchor similarity.

We conduct extensive experiments across multiple concept categories, including celebrities, artistic styles, nudity, and intellectual property. Our method achieves favourable trade-offs against prior approaches on Stable Diffusion v1.4, with additional results demonstrating broader applicability to FLUX.
